1. What does it mean to be a business in the UK?

Being or doing business can mean varying things, there are primarily three ways people go about doing this in the UK:

  • Individuals can do business by registering as a sole trader.
  • Individuals or groups can incorporate a company.
  • Groups can come together and register as a partnership.

Because of the variety of structures businesses can have, the majority of data and statistics use the Inter-Departmental Business Register (IDBR) and Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) codes to count and categorise businesses.

In a nutshell, the IDBR uses a variety of sources to create counts of businesses. Whilst SIC codes are used to categorise these businesses into an groups of activities.
