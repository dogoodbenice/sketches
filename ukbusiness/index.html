1. What does it mean to be a business in the UK?

Being or doing business can mean varying things, there are primarily three ways people go about doing this in the UK:

  • Individuals can do business by registering as a sole trader.
  • Individuals or groups can incorporate a company.
  • Groups can come together and register as a partnership.

Because of the variety of structures businesses can have, the majority of statistics use the Inter-Departmental Business Register (IDBR) to count businesses and the Standard Industrial Classification (SIC) to categorise businesses.

The SIC codes are used to group businesses into key activities. These have been split in to sections, divisions and groups.

The chart above shows Broad Industry Groups totalling the number of Standard Industrial Classifications attributed to them. The broad industry groups aren't highly used but are a helpful indication of how the UK economy is structured. Each of the SIC subsections can be explore interactively here.
