What did I learn when making this?

Aside from the main points, I also picked up these reflections:

  • Making charts responsive has room for improvement. Apologies if you read this on a mobile device and didn't have a great time.
  • Colours on charts matter. I probably still haven't got this quite right just yet either. From accessibility to conveying the information colour on charts matters ans needs to be thought out further.
  • Data is, thankfully, everywhere. Once you start searching it makes me glad the UK publishes and shares so much data openly.
  • It takes time to understand underlying statistical complexity. Whilst data is accessible the items and structure behind the figures takes time and contextual understanding to unpick.
  • Charting libraries are really powerful. Big thanks to all the creators and maintainers of those libraries who make creating and iterating on these charts really fast. Additional thanks to Tobias Ahlin for the intros into charting.

That's it, thanks for reading. If you have any feedback, requests for future articles or thoughts please do share it with me.
